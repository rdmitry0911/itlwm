Coverage scope
--------------
Single producer site: `postTahoeWclConnectCompleteEvent` in
`AirportItlwm/AirportItlwmV2.cpp`. This is the only place that
constructs and posts msg=0xd5 (APPLE80211_M_WCL_CONNECT_COMPLETE_EVENT)
in our kext, called only from `setLinkStateGated` on Tahoe when
linkState transitions to `kIO80211NetworkLinkUp`.

The diagnostic emits BEFORE the post and AFTER the post, plus a hex
dump of the 0xa4-byte payload sitting on the stack at the post site.
This allows Stage 2 evidence to compare wire bytes against the
AppleBCMWLAN reference layout decoded at
`analysis/cr230_applebcmwlan_sendConnectComplete_2026_04_29.txt`.

Diagnostic neutrality
---------------------
- No new state mutation; only XYLog calls + hex-dump reads of the
  on-stack payload buffer.
- No retry, fallback, packet substitution, queue manipulation, timing
  change, or behavioral side effect.
- Hex dump is bounded to the payload's 0xa4 bytes; XYLog emits 11
  fixed lines per post call.
- Because `postTahoeWclConnectCompleteEvent` itself is rate-limited by
  link-state transitions (fires once per LINK_UP event from
  `setLinkStateGated`), the diagnostic adds at most 11+2 = 13 log
  lines per LINK_UP event. Observed firing rate in CR-229 Stage 2
  evidence was ~10 events over 30 seconds (one per auto-join retry),
  so worst case is ~130 log lines per minute during connect retries —
  bounded.

Reference decomp
----------------
analysis/cr230_applebcmwlan_sendConnectComplete_2026_04_29.txt
documents the AppleBCMWLAN call chain (sendConnectComplete →
bulletinBoard → connectCompleteEventHandler → updateConnectCompleteEvent
→ handleJoinConnectComplete) and exactly which payload offsets the
framework reads:
  - payload[0..1]: event-level status (16-bit)
  - payload[2..3]: event-level reason (16-bit)
  - payload[0xc..0x13]: records[0].status + records[0].reason (64-bit)
  - bssid is NOT read by updateConnectCompleteEvent (validatePredicate
    at 0xffffff800220a5bc may use it, not yet decompiled).

Stage 2 evidence will compare the actual posted payload against the
expected zero-status/zero-reason success layout to determine whether
the issue is (a) wrong payload bytes, (b) post not reaching the
WCL subscriber, or (c) earlier state-machine gating.
